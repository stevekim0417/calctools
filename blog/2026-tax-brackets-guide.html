What Changed from 2025 to 2026

The IRS adjusts tax brackets annually for inflation to prevent "bracket creep" ‚Äî when inflation pushes you into a higher tax bracket even though your real purchasing power hasn't increased.

üìà Key Changes for 2026

  • Bottom two brackets (10% and 12%): Increased by ~4% due to OBBBA provisions
  • Higher brackets (22% - 37%): Increased by ~2.3%
  • Standard deduction: Up $350 for single filers, $700 for joint filers

The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA), passed in July 2025, made permanent most of the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (TCJA) provisions and added extra inflation protection for lower-income brackets.

How Tax Brackets Actually Work

A common misconception is that moving into a higher tax bracket means ALL your income gets taxed at the higher rate. This is not true.

The US uses a marginal tax system:

  1. Only the income within each bracket is taxed at that bracket's rate
  2. Your "effective tax rate" (total tax √∑ total income) is always lower than your top marginal rate
  3. Earning more money never results in less take-home pay

Frequently Asked Questions

When do the 2026 tax brackets take effect?

The 2026 brackets apply to income earned from January 1, 2026 through December 31, 2026. You'll file your 2026 tax return in early 2027.

Does my entire income get taxed at my highest bracket?

No! Only the portion of income within each bracket is taxed at that rate. This is called marginal taxation.

What's the difference between marginal and effective tax rate?

Marginal rate is the rate on your last dollar of income. Effective rate is your total tax divided by total income ‚Äî it's always lower than your marginal rate.
