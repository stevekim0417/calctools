What is Compound Interest?

Compound interest is interest calculated on both the initial principal AND the accumulated interest from previous periods. In simple terms: you earn interest on your interest.

The Rule of 72: Quick Mental Math

Want to know how long it takes to double your money? Use the Rule of 72:

Years to Double = 72 √∑ Interest Rate

Examples:

  • At 6% return: 72 √∑ 6 = 12 years to double
  • At 8% return: 72 √∑ 8 = 9 years to double
  • At 10% return: 72 √∑ 10 = 7.2 years to double

Factors That Boost Compound Interest

1. Higher Interest Rate

The difference between 5% and 8% might seem small, but over 30 years, $10,000 becomes $43,219 at 5% vs $100,627 at 8%. That's more than double!

2. More Frequent Compounding

Daily compounding beats monthly, which beats yearly. Most savings accounts compound daily; most investments effectively compound based on when you reinvest dividends.

3. Regular Contributions

Adding money consistently (like monthly 401k contributions) supercharges compound growth. Each new contribution starts its own compounding journey.

4. Time

The most powerful factor. You can't control markets, but you can start early and stay invested. Time in the market beats timing the market.

Compound Interest in Real Life

Where You Earn It

  • High-yield savings accounts: Currently 4-5% APY
  • Certificates of Deposit (CDs): Fixed rates, FDIC insured
  • Index funds: Historical average ~7-10% annually
  • 401(k) and IRA: Tax-advantaged compounding

Where You Pay It

  • Credit cards: 20%+ APR compounds against you
  • Student loans: Especially unsubsidized loans
  • Mortgages: Why extra payments save so much

‚ö†Ô∏è Compound Interest Works Both Ways

A $5,000 credit card balance at 22% APR, paying only minimums, takes 22 years to pay off and costs over $9,000 in interest. Compound interest working against you is devastating.

How to Get Started

  1. Start now. Even $50/month makes a difference over decades.
  2. Automate it. Set up automatic transfers to investment accounts.
  3. Don't touch it. Let compound interest work uninterrupted.
  4. Reinvest dividends. Turn off dividend payments; reinvest automatically.
  5. Increase contributions over time. As income grows, invest more.

Summary

Compound interest is simple but powerful:

  • You earn interest on your interest
  • Time is the most important factor
  • Starting early beats investing more later
  • Regular contributions accelerate growth
  • It works against you with debt

The best time to start investing was 20 years ago. The second best time is today.
