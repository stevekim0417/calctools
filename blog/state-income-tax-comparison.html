States With No Income Tax (2026)

Nine states do not levy a state income tax on wages and salaries. If minimizing your tax burden is a priority, these states offer significant savings:

üèÜ Zero Income Tax States

  • Alaska ‚Äì No income tax, plus residents receive annual oil dividend
  • Florida ‚Äì Popular for retirees, no tax on any income
  • Nevada ‚Äì No income tax, funded by gaming and tourism
  • New Hampshire ‚Äì No tax on wages (interest/dividends tax being phased out)
  • South Dakota ‚Äì No income tax of any kind
  • Tennessee ‚Äì No tax on wages (interest/dividends tax fully eliminated)
  • Texas ‚Äì No income tax, relies on property and sales tax
  • Washington ‚Äì No income tax on wages (has capital gains tax for high earners)
  • Wyoming ‚Äì No income tax, funded by mineral extraction
üí° Important Note: "No income tax" doesn't mean "no taxes." These states often have higher property taxes, sales taxes, or other fees to make up the revenue difference.

Beyond Income Tax: The Full Picture

When comparing states, income tax is just one piece of the puzzle. Consider these other factors:

Property Taxes

States without income tax often have higher property taxes. New Jersey and Illinois have the highest property tax rates in the nation, often exceeding 2% of home value annually.

Sales Tax

Combined state and local sales taxes can exceed 10% in some areas. Tennessee, Louisiana, and Arkansas have some of the highest combined rates.

Cost of Living

A higher tax rate in a low cost-of-living state might leave you with more purchasing power than a no-tax state with expensive housing (e.g., Texas cities vs. Tennessee).

Remote Work Considerations

If you work remotely, be aware of these complexities:

  • Tax nexus: Some states tax you based on where your employer is located
  • Reciprocity agreements: Neighboring states may have agreements to prevent double taxation
  • Convenience of employer rule: New York and a few other states may tax you even if you work from another state

‚ö†Ô∏è Moving for Tax Purposes

If you're relocating partly for tax reasons, ensure you truly establish residency. States aggressively audit "tax refugees" and require proof of permanent relocation‚Äîchange of driver's license, voter registration, and spending most of the year in the new state.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which state has the lowest taxes overall?

Alaska consistently ranks as having the lowest total tax burden, with no income tax, no state sales tax, and relatively low property taxes. Plus, residents receive an annual dividend from oil revenues.

If I move mid-year, which state do I pay taxes to?

Generally, you pay taxes to each state based on the income earned while residing there. You'll likely need to file part-year resident returns in both states.

Can my employer's state tax me if I work remotely?

It depends. Some states have "convenience of employer" rules that can tax remote workers. New York is particularly aggressive about this. Check your specific situation with a tax professional.

Are state taxes deductible on federal returns?

Yes, but the SALT (State and Local Tax) deduction is capped at $10,000 for federal purposes. This limits the federal tax benefit of living in a high-tax state.

Summary

State income taxes vary dramatically across the United States, from 0% in nine states to over 13% in California for high earners. When planning your finances or considering relocation, remember to look at the complete tax picture‚Äîincluding property taxes, sales taxes, and cost of living.
